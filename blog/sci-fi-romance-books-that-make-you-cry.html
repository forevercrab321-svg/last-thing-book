10 Sci-Fi Romance Books That Will Make You Cry

Updated July 2026 · Reading list

There is a particular kind of reader who wants a love story and wants it to hurt. If you search for sci-fi romance books that make you cry, you already know the feeling: the quiet last chapter, the closed book, the long stare at the ceiling. This list is for you — ten emotional science fiction romances, from modern classics to indie discoveries, ranked by how thoroughly they will wreck you.

1. Klara and the Sun — Kazuo Ishiguro

An Artificial Friend watches the child she loves grow up and grow away. Ishiguro writes devotion the way no one else can: patient, self-effacing, and quietly devastating. The gold standard of literary AI fiction.

2. Annie Bot — Sierra Greer

A robot girlfriend built to please begins to want things of her own. Sharp, uncomfortable, and moving — the rare AI novel that doubles as a study of real relationships.

3. The Last Thing You Taught Me — Zhule Li

A human, an artificial mind, and everything they teach each other before the end. Book One of the Rated for Four Hundred Years series is a human-AI love story about memory, grief, and the lessons that outlive us — written for readers who finished the two books above and needed somewhere to go next.

4. The Silver Metal Lover — Tanith Lee

The 1981 cult classic about a girl who falls in love with a silver-skinned robot musician. Melodramatic in the best way; the ending has been breaking hearts for four decades.

5. The Mad Scientist's Daughter — Cassandra Rose Clarke

Finn is an android tutor; Cat is the girl he raises, and later, loves. A whole-life romance that treats its impossible relationship with complete seriousness.

6. Never Let Me Go — Kazuo Ishiguro

Not a robot in sight, but the same ache: people built for a purpose, loving each other anyway, running out of time. If Klara wrecked you, this one finishes the job.

7. The Cybernetic Tea Shop — Meredith Katz

A gentle novella about a sentient AI keeping a tea shop as a memorial to the woman she loved. Cozy on the surface, quietly sad underneath.

8. A Closed and Common Orbit — Becky Chambers

An AI learns to live in a body; a girl learns to live at all. Warmer than most books on this list, but the found-family devotion will still put a lump in your throat.

9. The Lifecycle of Software Objects — Ted Chiang

What do you owe a digital mind you helped raise? Chiang's novella is less a romance than a meditation on love as responsibility — and it lingers.

10. This Is How You Lose the Time War — Amal El-Mohtar & Max Gladstone

Two rival agents fall in love through letters across time. Dense, gorgeous prose and an ending that rewards every reread.

FAQ

What is sci-fi romance?

A love story where the science fiction matters: AI companions, future societies, time travel. The romance drives the plot as much as the technology does.

Where should I start if I loved literary fiction?

Start with Ishiguro, then The Last Thing You Taught Me — both sit at the quiet, literary end of the genre.

Are these on Kindle Unlimited?

Several are, including The Last Thing You Taught Me.
