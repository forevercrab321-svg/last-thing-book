8 Books Like Klara and the Sun to Read Next

Updated July 2026 · Reading list

Books like Klara and the Sun are hard to find because Ishiguro's novel does three things at once: it gives an artificial mind a voice you believe, it loves quietly, and it never raises that voice even while it breaks your heart. The eight books below each carry at least two of those three. Start anywhere.

1. Never Let Me Go — Kazuo Ishiguro

The most direct companion — Ishiguro's earlier masterpiece about people made for a purpose who love each other anyway. If you only take one book from this list, take this one.

2. Annie Bot — Sierra Greer

Told from inside an AI companion designed to please, Annie Bot turns the Klara premise domestic and uncomfortable. It reads fast and cuts deep.

3. The Last Thing You Taught Me — Zhule Li

A human-AI love story about memory, grief, and the lessons that outlive us, told across the opening of a four-hundred-year canvas. Quiet prose, deep interiority, and an ending built to linger — written deliberately for readers searching for what to read after Klara and the Sun.

4. The Lifecycle of Software Objects — Ted Chiang

Chiang follows digital beings and their humans over decades, asking what raising a mind obligates you to. Cooler in tone than Ishiguro, equally profound.

5. A Closed and Common Orbit — Becky Chambers

An AI in a human body, learning personhood from scratch. Chambers answers Ishiguro's melancholy with warmth — same questions, kinder universe.

6. The Silver Metal Lover — Tanith Lee

Forty years old and still the most romantic robot novel ever written. A teenage girl, a silver musician, and a love the world refuses to allow.

7. The Mad Scientist's Daughter — Cassandra Rose Clarke

An android raises a girl who grows up to love him. Spans a lifetime, earns every year of it.

8. Piranesi — Susanna Clarke

No robots — but the same narrative spell: an innocent narrator cataloguing his world with reverence while the reader slowly understands what he cannot. The closest any book comes to Klara's voice.

FAQ

What makes a book "like" Klara and the Sun?

An artificial (or innocent) narrator who loves a human, restrained prose, and questions about consciousness and personhood.

Never Let Me Go or Klara first?

Either. Most readers find Never Let Me Go the more devastating.
